01 About

From aircraft wiring
to business strategy.

I spent six years at Boeing India solving some of the hardest engineering software problems in defense and aerospace — building compliance platforms for F-15 and F/A-18 aircraft, migrating satellite reporting systems, and architecting cross-domain PLM integrations in Siemens Capital. Every engagement was high-stakes, multi-stakeholder, and delivered with a US defense-program clock ticking.

The MBAEx at IIM Calcutta was a deliberate move — not away from technology, but toward the level where technology decisions are made. I wanted to understand the business models, the capital structures, and the strategic trade-offs that determine whether a technically excellent idea actually gets built at scale.

At Accenture Strategy, I bring both lenses. I'm the person who can walk into an aerospace or defense organisation, understand the engineering reality at depth, and translate it into strategic options for the boardroom. Long-term, I'm building toward something at the intersection of space technology, advanced manufacturing, and entrepreneurship.

Building toward

My career is a deliberate sequence. Six years of engineering depth in aerospace and defense. An MBA to understand business at scale. Consulting to see how strategy is actually executed across industries. And then — founding something.

The domain I keep coming back to: the intersection of advanced manufacturing, space technology, and digital infrastructure. India's space sector is opening up. Defense indigenisation is accelerating. The PLM and compliance infrastructure that large aerospace programs depend on is ripe for rethinking. I've lived inside these systems — I know where the gaps are.
